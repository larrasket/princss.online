Al-Masjid Al-Aqsa

Yliluoma states:

First of all, the Aya 17:1 makes no mention of Jerusalem. It just mentions a “distant Mosque”. Historically speaking, when this verse was given, there were no Islamic buildings in the lands of Israel. You read المسجد الاقصى‎ (al-Masjid al-Aqsa), and you immediately interpret it as al-Aqsa mosque in Jerusalem, because that is the name of a place you know, and that is how you have been taught to interpret it in this 22th century culture. The name was given to the Mosque that was built there by Umar, of the Rashidun caliphate, which came into existence after the death of Muhammad. In other words, it did not exist in the time of the supposed life of Muhammad. But you have to read the text in its historic context. You cannot put effect before cause. The name for a place (and the place itself) came long after the text. What did the words mean when the text was written?

This argument is based on a misunderstanding of the word “mosque” in Arabic, which is an adverbial of place (i.e. Ism al-Makan) an Ism Makan is any place to which that the stem verb of the word is applicable, for example the word “لعب” (La’ib), means play, if we add a “م” (Mim“) to get ”مَلعب“ (Mal’ab) we gen a place adverbial means ”the place where people can play“, so a mosque in Arabic, Masjid, means the place where people can practice the stem ”Sujūd."Mamdouh N. Mohammad, “Overview of Salat,” in Salat: The Islamic Prayer from a to Z (Dr. Mahmdouh N. Mohammad, 2003), 6–7. So basically what the Quran meant here is that there is a place that the prophet/people preformed a type of prayer there.

Another example of this naming is Masjid al-Haram (Mosque of Mecca, of Kabaa), people think of the stereotype of a Masjid when they hear the name but Masjid al-Haram was there even before the prophet, according to the Islamic beliefs the Haram mosque was built ages before the prophecy of Muhammad,John Esposito, Islam: The Straight Path, 3rd ed. (Oxford University Press, 1998). some Islamic scholars like Ibn al-Jawzi claims that it was even built by Adam (see his “Kitab Kashf al-Mushkil min Hadith al-Sahihayn, No. 360”).

Now Yliluoma is correct about that the name Aqsa means the distant, how we can tell that it is the same mosque in Palestine?

Well no ones actually questioned this because it was always very obvious. “We prayed toward Bait Al-Maqdis [Masjid of Jerusalem] with the Messenger of Allah for sixteen or seventeen months - Safwan was not sure - then it was changed to the Qiblah [Makkah].”

The prophet himself explicitly stated that the Mosque was in Jerusalem (Sahih Muslim No. 316):

I mounted it and came to the Temple (Bait Maqdis in Jerusalem) In despite of Sunnah.com’s parentheses, this is included in the original Arabic text (قَالَ فَرَكِبْتُهُ حَتَّى أَتَيْتُ بَيْتَ الْمَقْدِسِ). , then tethered it to the ring used by the prophets.
