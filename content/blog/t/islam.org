Sharia rulings fall into one of five categories known as “the five rulings”
(الأحكام الخمسة, al-aḥkām al-khamsa):

- farḍ/wājib (واجب / فرض) - compulsory, obligatory
- mustaḥabb/mandūb (مستحب) - recommended
- mubāḥ (مباح) - neutral, not involving God's judgment
- makrūh (مكروه) - disliked, reprehensible
- ḥarām/maḥzūr (محظور / حرام) - forbidden

A typical human does all of the 5, the prophet only commit Wajib, mandub, Jaez.

and Al-‘Uzzá are the ones that have taken away her sight." But she replied, "No,
by the house of Allah, you are lying. Al-Lat and Al-Uzza can neither harm nor

her eyesight, a healing that the Muslims attributed to Allah. However, the
Quraysh then said, "This is some of Muhammad's magic." When Abu Bakr saw she was
being tortured, he bought and freed her, along with her companion in slavery
Lubaynah. Her date of death is unknown, but she died after the Migration to
Madina.
